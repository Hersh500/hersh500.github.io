Projects

PCAP Parser

Based on what I learned at my internship work at Dell, I began working on software that could analyze network traffic files (packet capture files) and informatively display the gathered data. This would allow people to see where exactly their Internet traffic originates and potentially notice strange connections that could be the origin point of malware. I have published the complete code for the software on GitHub for anyone to improve upon or use in their own projects

A Novel Method for Cancer Detection

I partnered with a friend to develop a cheap & noninvasive way to detect cancer, using urinary nucleosides.We used the computational chemistry software Atomistix Toolkit to calculate current and other properties through the interface of a zigzag-structure graphene nanoribbon and these nucleosides. Our results suggest that each interface exhibited its own significantly unique current flow, showing that we could use a urine sample and measure the presence of the various nucleoside biomarkers, providing an excellent early detection system.

A Statistical Investigation into the Role of Genetic Diversity in Evolutionary Fitness-Related Traits

I created statistical models analyzing whether increased genetic diversity in sparrow populations was correlated with increased occurrences of advantageous physical traits during an internship at UC Santa Cruz. I wrote a research paper on the project and submitted it to the Siemens Discovery Competition
